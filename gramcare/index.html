Health Myth Buster

Scientific corrections for dangerous traditional practices in rural India.

FATAL DANGER

Snake Bite Treatment

✕ Myth: Suck out the venom or tie a tight tourniquet.

Sucking venom introduces mouth bacteria to the wound, does not remove venom, and poisons the rescuer. Tourniquets block arterial blood flow and often lead to limb amputations.

✓

Correct Action

Keep the victim completely still (movement spreads venom), keep the bitten limb below heart level, and go to the nearest PHC immediately for anti-venom.

HARMFUL

Burn Wounds

✕ Myth: Apply ghee, mustard oil, butter, or toothpaste.

Greasy substances and fats trap high heat inside the skin tissues, causing the burn to sink deeper. Toothpaste contains irritating chemicals that lead to severe tissue infection.

✓

Correct Action

Hold the burned area under clean, cool running water for 10-20 minutes. Cover loosely with a clean, dry, sterile cloth and visit a clinic.

FATAL DANGER

Fainting & Unconsciousness

✕ Myth: Force-feed water, onion juice, or hot tea.

An unconscious person cannot swallow safely. Forcing fluids or food down their mouth causes the contents to enter their lungs, leading to choking and asphyxiation.

✓

Correct Action

Place the person in the recovery position (on their side) to keep the airway open, loosen tight clothes, monitor breathing, and call emergency help.

FATAL DANGER

Open Wounds & Bleeding

✕ Myth: Apply mud, ash, or cow dung on open cuts.

Mud and cow dung contain dangerous spores of Clostridium tetani and other deadly pathogens, which cause severe sepsis and tetanus infections.

✓

Correct Action

Wash the wound with clean water and mild soap. Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth to stop bleeding and get a tetanus booster vaccine.
